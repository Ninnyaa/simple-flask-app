Among the many staples of Final Fantasy's gameplay is the iconic Job system, an involved progression system that lets you customize party members by selecting and developing them into specialized roles. While it is essentially a variation on Dungeons & Dragons' classic classes, the Final Fantasy games encourage you to swap between "jobs" as the situation demands. This system is utilized in FFXIV to great effect, though it is also modified to fit the Tank-Healer-DPS model that the gameplay is molded around.

Each combat discipline is designed to fill one of three major roles, be it tank (tasked with keeping enemies trained on them during scraps), healer (keeps party members healthy and the tank alive), and DPS (glass cannons that deal great damage, but cannot endure a direct onslaught of attacks). You can develop your character in whatever ranged or melee trade suits your fancy, and there is no penalty for swapping between jobs, save that you need to level up each individual discipline.

(Source Final Fantasy XIV Online (for PC) Review)
